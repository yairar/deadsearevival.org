• Scientific New Media Production

    The flagship project of our organization is the production of a scientific film and a first of-its-kind virtual reality 360 degree show, in cooperation with the Geography department of California State University and the Marine Science School in Haifa University.

    The goal of our project is to provide access to documentation of the decreasing level of the Dead Sea using advanced technology that allows us to provide an interactive user experience.

  

After mapping the coasts of the North-Western Dead Sea, the DSRP will focus on a huge field where unique geological phenomena occur in the Dead Sea - underwater "salt chimneys" created from underground fresh water streams to the Dead Sea. These phenomena were discovered scientifically only six years ago and our documentation proves the existence of microorganisms living in the “sea of death”.

  • We have a golden opportunity to document these salt chimneys emerging from the sea as a result of the decline in the sea level. Twice a month we will fly a professional drone (along with a 360-degree 5.7K quality camera) in a predetermined orbit around the “forest” of salt chimneys. The findings will be sent to the laboratories of the Department of Geography at the University of California, which will then process the documentation in order to create an accurate timeline and receive scientific data on the salt chimneys and the decline.

    The 360 degree visuals will be used in an interactive virtual reality show that will be suitable for display in museums, research institutes, cultural and educational centers. During the course of the work, preliminary findings will be sent to the leading international nature media channels, such as: (LOGOS) National Geographic, BBC EARTH, DISCOVERY
