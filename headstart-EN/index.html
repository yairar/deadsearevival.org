  • Dead Sea Revival Project invites you to join us on a journey to establish the Dead Sea Museum in Arad!

    Now funding the architectural design for the physical museum.

    Support for this project is a recognized donation in Israel (46a)

    We are proud and excited to introduce The Dead Sea Museum - an architectural masterpiece to be built in Arad (the gateway city to the Dead Sea) as an experiential venue for digital art and sculpture - a beacon of hope towards a global desire to save the Dead Sea.

    Your donations will enable us to entrust the design of the museum to the firm of Neumann Heiner Architects. They have already designed the virtual museum, which can already be enjoyed online.

    Inspiration for the concept of the Dead Sea Museum comes from the Guggenheim Museum in Bilbao, Spain, designed by Frank Gheary, and from the Niterói Museum in Brazil by Oscar Niemeyer, and the Louvre in Abu Dhabi.

    The goal of our project is to provide access to documentation of the decreasing level of the Dead Sea using advanced technology that allows us to provide an interactive user experience.

 
 
 
 
 

  • Arad was chosen for the future site of the museum because of its strategic location as the gateway city to the Dead Sea. This project will have tremendous socioeconomic benefits for its 25K residents and plans to be a center of inspiration for an active growing community of Dead Sea advocates. Once Launched, the Museum will join the other first-line museum in Israel, such as the Israel Museum, the Tel Aviv Museum of Art, the Land of Israel Museum etc., and give an interactive experience like the Bloomfield Museum in Jerusalem, the Madatech in Haifa, the Children’s Museum in Holon etc. The Museum plans to host 1M+ visitors a year, connecting them to the Dead Sea story through architecture, art, and the landscape.

    This campaign funds the designs and aims to form a strong community of Dead Sea lovers, as a strong proof of public support, that will facilitate the fund raising for the construction stage.

    Please take an active role today, support our campaign and be a part of our journey to bring new life to the Dead Sea.

 
 
 
 
 
 

  • The Dead Sea has rightfully won its international renown as The 8th Wonder of the World. It’s a touristic gem, as anyone who has ever toured the area (and/or participated in Midburn festival) can testify: David River, Arugot River, Zohar River, Kidron River, the Dead Sea Caves and Metzokei Dragot, Masada, Qumran, Qasr al-Yayud; Ovnat, Kibbutz Ein Gedi, KIbbutz Kalia, Kibbutz Mitzpe Shalem, Ovnat; Zohar, Megillot and Tamar regional councils, the beautiful Judea Desert - and the list goes on. It is a place of spirituality - from Masada and the Ancient Qumran sect and the Dead Sea Scrolls they have left us, and up to today, where it attracts spiritual tourism and ceremonies of all kinds, and hosts Christian Evangelist pilgrimage sites, promoting the fellowship between Christians and Jews. Now with the new Abraham Accords, we might see even more tourism coming in.

    The mineral and cosmetic industries, Ahava, Dead Sea factories (ICL), -417 cosmetics have also realized the potential of the area.

    However, the receding water level and shrinking of the Sea, has turned the place into a disaster area. Beautiful Mineral Beach, for example, was deserted after being destroyed by a sinkhole. The main reason is not so much the factories, but mostly because the Jordan River waters, coming from the Sea of Galilee, have been used for agriculture in the past decades, instead of nourishing the Dead Sea. Solutions have been come up and turned down, such as the Red-Dead canal (which could have been dangerous for the Red Sea).
